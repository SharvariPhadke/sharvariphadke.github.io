Leading an Inaugural Performance

This was my final annual college fest. I would soon be graduating. We were playing at the inauguration- in front of the entire college, deans, professors, guests and more. I was the lead playing the Tasha. There was a team of ~10 on the Indian Dhol. I had spent a month working with the Dhol leads and the team, orchestrating a performance set.

The entire set was synced to the beats of my instrument. I hit my first beats, and CRACK. My instrument, shattered. Facing an entire crowd, I felt all eyes on me. Some pitying, some judgemental. I couldn't go down like this. The replacement would take 10-15 minutes to arrive. I kept calm, my team did not panic. When the replacement arrived, we gathered ourselves.

We hyped up the crowd again, as if this was simply a minor setback. The performance set actually ended with rousing cheers from the crowd! It was quite an experience, but I learned to think on my feet, keep calm in the face of adversity, and actually turned it into an opportunity!
