Love: A Dream?
There she sat, sunflowers all around Under a tree, in the cool of the shade Thinking, emotions flowing Feelings she could not evade; Feelings, she thought, What could love be? Wind, rustling the leaves of the trees, The moon, causing waves in the sea? Love isn't a single emotion It's the feeling of home, The extra spoon of sugar in your tea A feeling; content, happy and warm The petrichor of the rain The crackle of the fire The care, the passion That made love a desire He was home, she thought A constant, her forever; She knew it was he, Whose existence made her better Twas a miracle, she thought For in a cosmos infinite She was drawn to him, Like a moth, to a flame well lit. From the moment they met She knew he was the one Her rock, her strength Her heart, he'd won. Dreamy gaze, teary eyes Waking up, I realise No dream was he For I was her, she was me.
Home
What if home was not a place, but just a moment in time? What if home was that instant Where everything was tranquil, calm, serene, An escape, from the typical arid routine. What if home was that moment That cold evening, that steaming hot chocolate Curled up fireside, nose deep in a book Snow settling on a quiet night, look! What if home was that moment That moment where, in life, you found Your passion, your one true purpose, The bourne of your being, no longer ambiguous? What if home was that moment With your soulmate, frozen in time Reminiscing for what feels like an aeon, Blissful, content, unwilling to move on? What if home wasn't just home But an avalanche of such musings If you were a leaf swaying in their breeze, Would you savor it, would you be at peace?
Rain
It's raining, gently pattering away The race of the raindrops Down the window, all foggy Twisting and turning, rushing down the glass, Glass, to the touch, cold and gloomy. Look a bit beyond; at the dew covered flower Feel its viridity, savour its beauty, The quiet pitter patter, just like music The rain, gently drumming on the leaf, like A gentle kiss on the hand, so to speak. Imagine a meadow, overgrown plants, waist-high Facing the sky, arms spread, palms drenched The cool breeze wafting through the puff sleeve The hem of the shirt, fluttering like a butterfly The setting sun, as noon turns to eve. Imagine a château, somewhere south of France Reading Shakespeare, engrossed, in a trance, On a plush couch, the tea, piping hot Steam windborne, like the dance of two fairies It's not just rain, it's nature's art. Imagine a lone beach, thunder rumbling, The wave crashing, bearing vengeance on the rock A bolt of lightning straight into the sea, A sudden surge of thrill down the spine, eyes wide, An overwhelming love for all that is earthly. Can any poem, any poet, ever do justice To the thrill of the thunder, the rage of the rain The calm before the storm, the peace after Will words simply ever be enough, to rightfully Romanticise the many moods of the weather?
